Session 0
Three days before Ascension Day, the board is already moving
Before the formal events of the campaign begin, the major players are already being positioned.
Three days before Ascension Day, Lysandra Vermont was arrested in the Vermont estate because a shipment of crystals, supposedly cut by her family, arrived at the Ministry depleted.
That is treated as:
* Grand Larceny
* Possession of Unsanctified Material
The second charge means possessing divine material one should not have.
At the same time, Caspian Talus Dryce was maneuvered into sounding heretical in public. He did not openly preach heresy, but he was pushed and framed into appearing to argue that the Divine, though divine, could still be manipulated through earthly means such as dirt and iron.
That is the political backdrop before the session formally begins.
________________


Zaren Dryce’s Intro
Solaris before Ascension
The day begins on the morning of Ascension Day, at 9:00 AM, before the processions of the Ascension Festival, which are held at exactly high noon.
But instead of Cyritium, the first scene opens in Solaris.
The location is the Zaren Dryce Estate, specifically the innermost sanctum.
The Dryce family is one of the largest in Solaris, but not a geographically unified one. It is a fractured family spread across many estates, many branches, and many moving parts.
Zaren’s inner sanctum is an anomaly in the city.
Outside, Solaris screams with the high-frequency hum of aether crystals as the city prepares for the festivities.
Inside, behind three feet of metal-lined stone, there is only:
* the ticking of clocks
* the crackle of candles
* the smell of old parchment
* the faint metallic scent of polishing oil
Zaren sits at the center of this artificial silence.
________________


Zaren and his parents remember a different sky
Zaren Dryce is fifty-five years old, gray-haired, scarred, and still in strong condition.
He has the presence of an old veteran policeman.
His family has always loved the guards. Some of his ancestors wore military boots, but none reached the rank he did.
He bears two tattoos:
* one on his cheek, carried since he was eighteen
* another on his forehead, gained later as he rose through the ranks
He is not alone in the sanctum.
His elderly parents are there with him, both around eighty years old, old enough to seem almost ghostlike.
His father, his voice reduced to a rasp, rests a weak hand on him and says:
Zaren’s Father:
 “Listen to the city. This isn’t progress. It’s a funeral march. They’re celebrating the Ascension. But we remember the sky.”
His mother sits nearby with tea that has long gone cold and asks:
Zaren’s Mother:
 “What is your burden tonight, son Are you working the lines”
Zaren says yes. He has to be there.
She says they understand his burden and do not want to add to it.
This immediately establishes something crucial about the Dryce household:
they are not aligned emotionally with the official celebration.
They remember something older.
________________


Zaren’s daughter and the family ritual
Then the door opens without knocking.
A little girl, about ten or eleven years old, with short orange hair bursts in.
She hugs her grandparents, climbs into Zaren’s lap, and excitedly tells him that her mother says she is getting her spike today.
She says it will make her part of the great song.
She wants a new violet dress to match the light.
Zaren agrees to take her.
She asks why his room is so dark when the lamps outside are so beautiful.
Zaren explains that this is a peaceful room. It is for finding peace inside oneself, not for light and festival display.
Then she remembers something important:
someone from work is waiting outside for him.
She has forgotten the name.
________________


Julius Mccord enters the sanctum
Zaren steps outside and finds Auditor General Julius Mccord waiting at the door.
His mere presence seems to drop the temperature by ten degrees.
He is known as the Voice of the Light.
He wears an elegant white-and-gold uniform and a silver half-mask, marking him as officially on duty.
He is completely alone.
That alone signals serious business.
He greets Zaren:
Julius Mccord:
 “Under the Light, Marshal Dryce.”
Then he steps inside and takes in the candles, clocks, and parchments.
He wastes no time.
He says that the Templar’s Office does not care about calendar convenience, even on Ascension Day.
Then he tells Zaren:
a high-importance asset is scheduled for transfer tomorrow.
He asks about the readiness of Zaren’s personal platoon.
Zaren says they are always ready.
Mccord asks about their deployment.
Zaren explains:
* two commanders serve under him in the royal guard
* one has been sick, but will be ready if needed
* one team is currently undercover on an urgent investigation
* he can still provide fifteen men and even his second-in-command if necessary
Mccord answers with a direct order.
Zaren is to assemble:
* a squad of his most trusted men
* including himself
* men who understand that the Radiant Law is the only thing that matters
They are to depart for Cyritium at last light and report by first light the next morning to the Office of the Holy Order.
Mccord says:
Julius Mccord:
 “The Empire is breathing, Marshal. Do not let it choke on your watch.”
Then he leaves immediately.
The assignment is not framed as optional.
________________


Zaren prepares his family, and the real danger enters
Zaren turns to his daughter and tells her they will have to move quickly. He will stay with her and her mother for the next two hours, then he must go.
As he turns back, his father signals for him to return to the sanctum.
When he enters, he realizes a third person has been there all along, leaning against a shelf.
It is Asmodeus Vein.
He is the man Zaren commissioned fifteen years ago to build the house, and he is connected to Zaren’s parents.
Asmodeus says:
Asmodeus Vein:
 “The tide is coming in. And it’s carrying salt this time. And the flame will not drown.”
Then he asks whether Zaren still likes his work, whether he still likes this room, and whether it is holding fast.
Zaren says the room is due for maintenance, then tells him they need to talk.
Asmodeus sits.
He explains that they have been hearing noise from the capital. Something is moving inside the high houses.
The details are unclear, but one thing is certain:
if Julius Mccord is personally handpicking Zaren for an escort, this is not a promotion.
It is a trap.
________________


The Brotherhood wants the transport sabotaged
Asmodeus says the Brotherhood believes one of these assets is carrying something foundational.
He says they cannot allow that asset to reach wherever the Ministry wants it taken.
They need to sabotage the transport.
He explains that the Brotherhood believes this truth could crack the façades of the high houses.
Then he makes the demand explicit:
they need the transport stopped.
Zaren asks for specifics.
Does Asmodeus know anything about the assets Size, sex, number
Asmodeus says he will try to get more intel by tomorrow.
Zaren immediately objects:
tomorrow will be too late.
He asks whether the intel can come by tonight or first thing in the morning, and whether a replica could be prepared so that something is delivered rather than nothing.
This matters because Zaren is already thinking like a man trying to satisfy both the Ministry and the Brotherhood without openly betraying either.
Asmodeus answers that if the Ministry is personally assigning Zaren, then the cargo is important enough that it must not reach its destination.
Then he produces tools for sabotage:
* a small disc
* two prisms
He tells Zaren to use them on the aether crystals of whatever transport is provided.
These devices will drain the charge in minutes.
Once the transport stops, the Brotherhood will be waiting in the dark to intercept.
Asmodeus promises support and says the Brotherhood will make sure Zaren’s men are not harmed, but the assets must be extracted and taken to Brotherhood safe houses before the Ministry realizes what happened.
________________


Zaren tries to keep control of the mission
Zaren refuses to accept a plan that simply wrecks the transport and leaves him ruined.
He says he cannot fail at something this large, not if it may sabotage them in the future.
He asks instead whether there is any undetectable tracking method.
Asmodeus says no. Magic like a Null Sigil would be trackable by the Ministry as well.
So Zaren proposes another plan:
have one of the closest brothers ready to be taken hostage.
Someone who will not crack.
Asmodeus pushes back, warning that if the Ministry gets such a person, they will eventually break them.
Still, he agrees to think.
He tells Zaren to keep the prisms just in case.
Zaren promises that something will be delivered rather than nothing.
Asmodeus reaches out to shake his hand and says:
Asmodeus Vein:
 “The flame will not drown.”
That seals the understanding.
By the end of this scene, Zaren is trapped between:
* his oath and position in the Guilded Guards
* the direct attention of Julius Mccord
* the expectations of the Brotherhood
* and the knowledge that his family is quietly aligned with the warning, not with the Ministry

Alex Talus’s Intro
House Talus in panic
The scene shifts to the Talus Estate, one day after the Gala of the First Crown.
The household is in chaos.
Alex Talus is in her private room, which appears clinically perfect, perhaps the cleanest room imaginable.
But that perfection is false.
With her are two cousins:
* Aria Talus
* Halia
Aria is pale, trembling, holding a paper, and crying.
She tells Halia that they took Caspian in the middle of the Beacon Ignition.
Not only did they arrest him, they erased him.
She says the Ministry is already at the cutting shops, siphoning the vaults, and saying that the Talus name is stained by the shadow of sedition.
Halia tries to calm her and says Caspian is not really part of their family anyway.
Aria snaps back that it does not matter.
The Auditor General is here.
Their father is on his knees downstairs.
The family needs a sacrifice to prove it is not part of Caspian’s “logic.”
They are looking for someone expendable.
That is the emotional state of the house before Alex is even summoned.
________________


Julius Mccord corners the Talus family
The scene shifts to the grand foyer below.
There, Julius Mccord stands alone, addressing Gerald Talus, Alex’s father.
He reminds Gerald that the Ministry holds most of House Talus’s contracts.
Gerald corrects him: 74%.
He proudly describes Talus precision in reagents, alchemy, and the maintenance of the High Spires’ flawless glow.
Mccord leans in and says a flaw has been detected.
Caspian has stained their ledgers.
His ideas are contagion.
The Ministry is commissioning a transport to remove the infection and now requires a Talus monitor to accompany the shipment, to ensure the transport crystals do not suffer harmonic overload.
He presents it as a simple field internship, perhaps for Gerald’s daughter or one of her cousins. A chance for House Talus to prove devotion to stability rather than sedition.
Gerald tries to defend Alex by saying she is a researcher, a student of the Light.
Mccord cuts him off.
He does not care who she is.
Since Gerald mentioned her, he says, she is exactly who they need.
She will find the Penance a most illuminating classroom.
The choice is not really a choice.
________________


Gerald breaks and forces Alex to go
Gerald rushes upstairs to Alex, throws the other girls out, closes the door, and grabs her by the shoulders.
He explains what is at stake.
The vibration in the walls, the hum of the aether crystals, the life of the house itself, all of it depends on Talus remaining in favor.
If the Ministry pulls even one of their contracts, the hum stops.
And when the hum stops, they cease to exist.
He reminds Alex that she saw what happened to House Vermont:
they did not merely arrest Lysandra. They turned her siblings into ward stewards, prisoners in their own home, while House Aurelian siphoned their legacy.
He refuses to let that happen to House Talus.
He says he will not watch his daughters wearing Null Sigils because of Caspian’s arrogance.
Then he gives Alex the role directly.
This “internship” is not an internship.
It is a blood sacrifice to prove the family is still in line.
Alex is the only one with the right credentials who is not already monitored.
She knows the crystal work better than anyone.
She has studied under Caspian.
She is the only one who can satisfy the Ministry and stabilize the transport.
He hands her the paper and orders her:
* report to Marshal Zaren
* act as the technical liaison for the transport
* keep his crystals stable
* do whatever it takes to ensure the shipment reaches its destination
* become the reason the Talus name stays in the light
Then he gives her one final practical instruction:
stick close to Zaren.
He will protect her.
The paper formally instructs her to report to Marshal Zaren Dryce and work under him until the journey is complete.
________________


Where Session 0 leaves the board
By the end of Session 0, the key lines of tension are already in place:
* Lysandra Vermont has been set up and erased by forged crystal crime
* Caspian Talus Dryce has been politically framed as a seditionist and magical contaminant
* Zaren Dryce has been chosen by the Ministry for a sensitive escort while simultaneously pressured by the Brotherhood to sabotage it
* Alex Talus has been forced into the role of technical monitor to preserve House Talus’s standing
The transport that will become the center of Session 1 is now fully prepared as a convergence point.
Everyone is being moved toward the same ship for different reasons:
* punishment
* cover-up
* loyalty test
* sabotage
* survival
And none of them yet knows how completely those motives are about to collide.
