Session 1
Session 1
The Sanctification Cells
The session opens in the Sanctification Cells, a temporary holding facility beneath the Ministry of Sanctification.
More specifically, the party is in the Dampening Chamber, built by House Vein.
The air smells sharply of chlorine. Even underground, the hum of the High Spire above is unmistakable, because the aether crystals inside it are enormous.
Inside the only occupied cage sits a brown-haired girl with dirty hands and clothes.
This is Lysandra Vermont.
Her life was unmade three days ago.
She sits on the floor, trying to process what has happened to her.
Her thoughts churn:
* she feels betrayed
* she feels helpless
* she is thinking about her siblings
* she is trying to understand who did this to her family
* she is certain that someone forged the signature
* someone wants to take their place
* someone wants the Vermonts erased
She also remembers cutting gems, and knows that the gems they found empty bore the Vermont facet, even though she never personally saw the box.
That memory matters because it suggests that even before her arrest, she already had reason to suspect a deliberate setup.
________________


Lady Vermont comes in secret
The only door to the chamber usually opens once every twenty-four hours for a guard to bring food and water.
But suddenly it grinds open at the wrong time.
Instead of a guard, a woman enters in black silk with her face covered like a mourner at a funeral.
It is Lady Vermont, Lysandra’s mother.
Her face is heavy with grief.
Behind her enters a High Templar in a silver full-mask. He whispers something in her ear and then steps outside to wait.
Lady Vermont looks at her daughter and says:
Lady Vermont:
 “You look like a common debtor, Lysandra. What happened with you”
Lysandra answers sharply:
Lysandra:
 “You tell me first what happened.”
Her mother tells her the scale of the disaster.
Their house is occupied. Her siblings are prisoners in their own home. She herself is not even allowed to say the name Vermont aloud.
She asks Lysandra what she has done.
Lysandra insists she has done nothing.
Someone betrayed them.
She begs her mother for help:
* go back
* search for papers
* find anything related to her father
* look for anything that might explain what is happening
Lady Vermont says the Ministry has told her that Lysandra’s father may be involved. She asks whether his actions are chasing the family from the grave.
Lysandra insists he is innocent.
Lady Vermont laments how hard they worked to avoid ever falling this low, then asks what Lysandra needs.
Lysandra repeats that she needs information.
Her mother then gives her the worst practical news:
she will not be able to come back.
The Ministry is moving her.
When Lysandra asks where, Lady Vermont says she does not know.
Then, checking that the door is closed, Lady Vermont leans in and secretly pulls something from her pocket.
She has brought Lysandra her jeweler’s tools.
She says perhaps Lysandra can find solace in them here.
Lysandra thanks her.
Then Lady Vermont tells her something far stranger and more important.
She says there is a reason the Vermont facet never flickers.
It was not only her father’s skill.
Before Lysandra was born, her father went into a dark place, met a king, and made a deal.
He invited the light to sit where his heart used to be.
Lady Vermont calls it a burden of inheritance.
She says that king did not wear a crown.
She does not believe Lysandra’s father signed the ledgers.
Then she says she will try to get Lysandra out, though the odds are terrible, and leaves.
This is one of the first major revelations of the campaign:
the Vermont line is tied to some inherited bargain involving light, a king, and a heart replaced by something else.
________________


Caspian is thrown into the neighboring cell
As Lady Vermont leaves, boots and voices sound outside.
Two Guilded Guards enter dragging an unconscious man across the floor by his feet.
His Researcher Prime robes are torn.
Both hands are branded with two glowing Null Sigils on the back.
That alone is extraordinary, since one Null Sigil is already severe. Two means the Ministry was being exceptionally careful.
The guards throw him into the adjacent cell, lock it, turn the key, and deliberately kick the cage so Lysandra will watch.
Then they leave.
The outer iron door locks.
Lysandra is left completely alone with the new prisoner.
This is Caspian Talus Dryce.
He has not eaten, drunk, or rested properly for twenty-four hours. He has been moved repeatedly from place to place with his head covered, as the Ministry tried to lose a tail before finally bringing him here.
When he wakes, he feels terrible.
All of his belongings have been stripped from him.
The first thing he does is check the golden tattoo he drew on his arm.
It is still there.
That matters, because it is not merely decoration. He secretly built function into it.
He looks up and sees Lysandra, who has been trying to wake him by throwing things at him.
Lysandra recognizes him before he recognizes her.
Lysandra:
 “Oh look, I know you. I know you. How come you are here”
Caspian answers warily:
Caspian:
 “Thank my lucky stars. Who are you, my dear What brought you here”
Lysandra says they arrested her and insists it is not her fault. She identifies herself as being from House Vermont and asks if he knows them.
Caspian immediately asks the real political question:
Caspian:
 “Which house has taken over”
Lysandra says she still does not know.
Caspian then asks whether she has heard of House Aurelian.
She says yes.
This is the first signal that Caspian already suspects noble-house maneuvering behind the case.
________________


Caspian secretly rebuilds a toolkit from his own skin
While they speak, Caspian starts scraping the golden tattoo off his arm.
This is not self-harm or panic.
He is secretly disassembling a tool kit he had ingeniously tattooed into his own skin, interlocking the pieces so that the bronze tattoo can be turned back into thieves’ tools.
He cannot use magic because of the Null Sigils, so he is already adapting.
That detail is very important for understanding Caspian:
even stripped, tracked, exhausted, and caged, he is still building.
________________


Julius Mccord condemns them both
Then Auditor General Julius Mccord enters.
He is followed by two guards wearing the uniform of a man already in the room, someone hidden or invisible in one of the cages.
Mccord wears a silver half-mask and carries a ledger.
He leans in and whispers something to the hidden man, who replies.
Then Mccord turns to Lysandra and Caspian and says:
Julius Mccord:
 “I told you not to seek defense. Right You were told. Your family cannot save you. You have stolen from the Ministry.”
Lysandra protests that she did not.
Then Mccord turns to Caspian and attacks him differently.
He says many people think Caspian is brilliant, but they know the truth.
They say it was all a fluke of luck.
Then he pronounces sentence:
Julius Mccord:
 “Caspian Talus Dryce, Lysandra Vermont, you are unfit. You are stripped of your names. You are condemned to permanent exile to the continent of Vastraya. You are to be transported on board the Penance.”
That is the formal beginning of their exile.
________________


Zaren arrives at the docks
Meanwhile, Marshal Zaren Dryce arrives in Cyritium at dawn.
His team, an elite fifteen-man platoon, is in top shape, fully equipped, and well-rested.
A junior auditor tells him only the practical details:
* be at the docks at 6 PM
* wait for a technician
* no further information is given
At the docks, the air is thick with the static of arriving ships.
The ships are powered by massive aether crystals, and when many gather close together, their crystals resonate badly and large-form crystals lose charge.
That is why the ships are parked far apart.
The hum of the crystals is intense.
Zaren is placed in charge of the ship, alongside:
* his fifteen men
* five navigators from House Castrell
Then a young technician arrives carrying a bag and hands Zaren her deployment papers.
This is Alex Talus.
She boards the ship and immediately notices that the aether crystals are installed directly into the mast, both above and below deck. The ship carries no sails. It is powered entirely by those crystals, which she has been assigned to monitor.
________________


Zaren takes command of the ship
Zaren begins sweeping the vessel and issuing orders.
He asks whether the shipment has already arrived on board.
He is told:
* the shipment is already aboard
* it came with three individuals and a general auditor wearing a silver mask
* the three who delivered it have already left
* the navigators and crew are on ship
* only Captain Castrell is still awaited
Zaren gives a precise order:
everything must be ready by 6:50. The ship moves at 7:00.
Soon, a man approaches wearing a captain’s hat and a short feathered cape.
He looks nothing like a typical polished House Castrell noble. He is unkempt, not clean-shaven, and resembles a civilian sailor far more than a military officer.
Still, his papers prove that he is the captain.
________________


The hold of the Penance
Zaren goes down into the hold.
There he finds Caspian and Lysandra sitting on a bench, bound hand and foot to the floor.
Behind them are three massive wooden crates, secured with straps.
Zaren and Caspian immediately recognize each other. They played together fifteen years ago when they were young.
Zaren maintains a completely neutral face and says aloud:
Zaren:
 “Peculiar situation we find ourselves in.”
At the same time, he sends a telepathic message directly into Caspian’s mind:
Do you know about the crew that House Aurelian are staging
Caspian uses Detect Thoughts to receive and process it, reading not only the message but Zaren’s tactical awareness of the room:
* two prisoners
* three crates
* two exits
* the structure of the ship
* how the ship is being guarded
Caspian responds telepathically:
So you know they are being shipped to the continent, and if Zaren was chosen to be here, then he too is being cast out.
The alliance is not explicit yet, but the old recognition is already turning into covert coordination.
________________


The ship departs
A crew member comes down and tells Zaren it is time to move.
The ship begins to vibrate.
Above deck, the captain takes hold of a small steering device, and the ship veers away from the dock into open water.
With his arcane expertise, Caspian immediately notices how inefficient the ship’s crystal array is. It interacts poorly with the surrounding ships’ resonance.
That observation matters because Caspian cannot stop analyzing systems, even in chains.
Zaren whistles, and his mechanical bird familiar flies down to him.
At the same time, Caspian reaches upward mentally again and reads the drunk captain’s surface thoughts.
What he senses is not confidence.
It is fear.
The captain’s most prominent thought is:
“The land of the law is three days behind us.”
That phrase paints the voyage as a threshold.
They are not just leaving port.
They are leaving legality, order, and recognized civilization.
________________


The captain leans in, and Caspian finishes the lockpicks
Later, down in the hold, the captain leans in close to Caspian and says:
Captain:
 “They give you the special treatment. Never seen one with two.”
He is referring to the two Null Sigils.
In the darkness, Caspian finishes assembling his lockpicks.
The session closes on that image:
* Lysandra has learned her family carries an inherited secret tied to a bargain with light
* Caspian has been stripped, branded, and condemned, but is already rebuilding tools from his own skin
* Zaren has taken command of the ship carrying them into exile
* Alex has boarded as the crystal technician
* and the Penance is now underway toward Vastraya
