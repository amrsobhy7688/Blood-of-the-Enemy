Session 2
Shipwreck, awakening, and the first Refracted
The session opens in the aftermath of disaster.
The party’s ship exploded, and they were thrown onto the shore. As they regained consciousness, they heard the sounds of fighting.
When they fully woke, they found themselves facing strange creatures: the Refracted.
The Refracted looked like ordinary humans in normal clothes, except as if they were dead. Glass shards protruded from their bodies.
After killing them, the party encountered Mara of the Iron Will on the shore. She told them she needed a short rest.
________________


Mara rests while the party regroups
While Mara tried to recover, the party briefly split up.
* Zaren Dryce stayed with Mara and made sure she was okay.
* Lysandra Vermont and Caspian Talus Dryce went off for about half an hour, searching the ground for supplies or anything useful.
By the time they returned, Mara had mostly caught her breath, though she had not truly completed a short rest.
When Lysandra returned, she looked badly shaken. Her hair was disheveled, and there was blood on her face.
Caspian looked normal.
Lysandra also had a small black cat walking beside her. The cat had silver eyes.
________________


Zaren studies Mara while they are alone
During the time alone with her, Zaren notices several unsettling details about Mara.
She is deeply exhausted, but her body does not look like it should be capable of this level of fatigue.
The reason soon becomes visible:
the glass protrusions are coming from her own body.
Zaren also notices something strange about her equipment. She carries:
* two daggers
* a shortsword
* a longsword on her back
* a lance
It is an unusual combination of weapons for one person.
________________


Mara explains the land
When the group is together again, Mara immediately warns them about Vastraya.
Mara of the Iron Will:
 “This place, this land here in Vastraya, is hungry and wrong.”
She tells them that their skin is burning and their minds are in static. If they remain like this, they will become Hollow.
They do not yet know what that word means.
Mara explains that the Hollow are beings of glass and resentment wandering the shores, like the creatures they just fought.
It is obvious from the way she speaks that she is deeply disgusted by them.
________________


The ringing in the land
The effects of the land are already beginning to take hold.
Zaren notices a constant ringing in his ears.
Alex Talus feels the same ringing, but with an extra layer of discomfort, like the sound is making her grind her teeth without realizing it.
Mara explains:
Mara:
 “That bone-deep ache, that ringing, is the scream of the land. It is hungry for you.”
Then she tells them what they need to survive the night:
Mara:
 “If you want to survive this night, you will do it with prayers or perfume. You will need the glass.”
________________


Zaren and Alex are sent to gather shards
Mara sends Zaren and Alex to search the area for shards of glass large enough to use.
She warns them clearly:
Mara:
 “Do not touch them with your bare hands. The glass is strange. It will seek you out and bite you. It will feed until there is nothing left.”
The warning is literal, not metaphorical.
________________


A coded exchange between Mara and Zaren
While the others search, Zaren kneels near Mara and asks whether there is anything he can do to make her more comfortable.
Mara studies him and replies with a coded question:
Mara:
 “You are a follower of Elidria I usually get these right. What do you follow Which of them”
Zaren answers in kind, identifying himself with the Winged Watch path and confirming that he is a follower of the Sovereign Quadrants.
Then he says:
Zaren:
 “The tide is coming.”
Mara replies:
Mara:
 “But the seal will hold. I think you will find that the caverns suit you more. No help is needed from your kind.”
This exchange clearly signals that the two of them recognize something about one another’s religious or factional alignment, even if the full meaning is not yet explained.
________________


Two shards are recovered
Alex finds a long, thin piece of black glass.
Remembering Mara’s warning, she uses cloth to pick it up rather than touching it directly.
Zaren finds another shard, nearly circular like a coin, with purple smoke moving inside it.
They bring both shards back to Mara.
________________


Mara bonds the party to glass
Mara tells them bluntly:
Mara:
 “You would not survive without the glass. I need to bond you.”
She asks who will go first.
Zaren volunteers.
Mara takes the circular shard from him and handles it easily in her bare hands. Then she grips his shin.
At that moment, Zaren notices something crucial:
Mara’s nails are themselves glass, as if they are naturally part of her body.
She drives one of those glass nails into his leg, peeling the skin down. The pain is severe. Then she presses the shard into the wound.
As soon as it touches him, Zaren feels suddenly heavy, as though he has gained ten kilos. The shard seems to search for the right position inside the wound, dragging itself until it settles at a strange angle inside his leg. Then it glows purple.
Mara looks at him and says:
Mara:
 “Sorry, lord. You’ll never dance again.”
Then she adds:
Mara:
 “If it were for me, I would have left you here. But I know that if you came back as Refracted, I would not enjoy killing you. What you follow is naught but torment.”
________________


Alex chooses the ear
Alex offers her own shard and says she wants it placed in her ear.
Mara agrees.
She pierces Alex’s ear with a glass nail and threads the long black shard through it. Alex’s ear splits and bleeds as the shard bends and wraps itself into a black ring around her right ear.
Mara tells her:
Mara:
 “This one is hungry for light, but it will help you in this dark.”
________________


Lysandra and Caspian are also bonded
Lysandra is bonded as well. Her shard is yellow.
Caspian is also meant to be bonded, but Mara cannot do it while the Null Sigil restraints are still on his hands.
So she removes them first.
To remove Caspian’s Null Sigils, Mara takes out a much smaller crystal, older and weaker-looking. She asks for his hand, and he gives it.
She begins reciting the Prayer of Ignition, part of the Song of Resonance, but Lysandra notices that the prayer is full of curses against Solaria.
Mara is mocking him while she works.
Then she takes out an iron spike-like tool and drives it through his hand to remove the sigil.
Only after that does Caspian receive a gray shard with swirling smoke between two fingers.
He had first found a mint-colored shard, but Mara stopped him and kept that one for herself, telling him:
Mara:
 “No. This one is for me. Let me find another one for you.”
________________


The effects of the bonding
After the bonding is complete, the party learns the effects.
Alex Talus
* maximum HP reduced by 5 while the shard remains in her body
* disadvantage on Constitution saving throws against disease and poison
* in dim light or darkness, she can hide more effectively
Zaren Dryce
* maximum HP reduced by 5
* disadvantage on Constitution saving throws against disease and poison
* attempts to move him against his will are made at disadvantage
* because of the shard in his leg, he automatically fails Acrobatics checks
These effects make it clear that the glass is not a clean blessing. It preserves life, but always at a cost.
________________


Mara refuses to explain on the shore
After the bonding, Alex asks Mara:
Alex:
 “How did you get here”
Mara answers:
Mara:
 “This is not the place for stories. Let us reach Hold Mount. I will explain there.”
Then she asks Alex:
Mara:
 “Are you a follower of Lunaria or Solaria”
Alex answers that she is neither.
Mara replies:
Mara:
 “Good on you. We are alike in that.”
Then she adds:
Mara:
 “This earth here is hungry. We cannot stem the obsidian sand. Let me take you to Hold Mount.”
________________


The march toward Hold Mount
The group begins moving with Mara along the shore toward Hold Mount.
Zaren asks whether they can pass by the place where the waves break, in case they can salvage anything from the wreck.
Mara answers:
Mara:
 “Everything breaks at Hold Mount. If you lost something, you will find it on its shore.”
Then she warns them:
Mara:
 “Remember: a single link fails one of you, and two see the day. We move as a chain.”
This reflects the same chain-and-link worldview already hinted at in her earlier coded exchange with Zaren.
________________


Mara kills Caspian’s familiar instantly
As they walk, Caspian sends out his familiar to scout.
The moment it rises from his shoulder, Mara whirls with terrifying speed and throws a dagger, killing it instantly.
She says:
Mara:
 “You need to tell me before you do these kinds of things.”
Caspian asks why she reacted that way, pointing out that there are no visible animals or birds here.
Mara replies:
Mara:
 “The reason there are no animals here is because this place is wild. If it is flying, it is a predator or food.”
The reaction is not just practical. It is instinctive, immediate, and violent.
________________


Zaren sees fear beneath Mara’s anger
As they continue, Zaren asks why Mara carries daggers if she fears flying things so much.
He notices, through insight, that her reaction was not simple anger.
It was terror first, then grief or guilt, almost as though she had lost the dagger rather than merely thrown it.
That detail strongly suggests a deeper history behind her reflexes.
________________


More truths about life in Vastraya
Mara explains more as they travel.
Life here is difficult.
A person can be hunted by:
* the Refracted
* scavengers
* other survivors
* people very much like the party themselves
When asked whether everyone here is a shipwreck survivor, Mara says very little and remains guarded.
When asked whether all of her companions are shipwreck survivors or whether some are native to the land, she strongly implies that the natives are not to be trusted and that life in Vastraya teaches you not to pry into other people’s affairs.
When asked why she herself is here, Mara answers simply:
Mara:
 “Because we were betrayed.”
________________


Zaren asks about the Captain
Zaren:
 “We were betrayed by our captain.”
Mara replies with sharp contempt:
Mara:
 “Nice to meet you, Zaren. You speak too much for a very wise person, and you do not seem very bright. In Vastraya, we help each other survive. You are my friend until you are my enemy.”
This is a pointed response. She accepts the introduction, but she is already reading him critically.
________________


Lysandra’s cat becomes something else
As they continue, Lysandra’s black cat begins shifting in strange ways.
At one point it becomes a toddler-like child, around a year and a half old, with slightly yellow skin.
Zaren can tell it looks human-shaped, but not truly human.
The transformation happens without Lysandra casting anything or taking visible action.
Mara does not react with fear to the cat or the toddler form, even though she instantly killed Caspian’s flying familiar.
She seems confused, but not threatened.
That contrast matters.
________________


The coast, the tide, and the dead land
The group continues along the coast, slightly inland.
The black sand crunches underfoot like obsidian and quartz.
The land is dead and burnt.
The tide behaves unnaturally, receding and returning much too quickly.
When Zaren asks whether the hunters come from the sky, Mara answers:
Mara:
 “Some come on the ground. Others come in the sky.”
That confirms the danger is not limited to ground level.
________________


The wreck that looks like a structure
Eventually, as they approach Hold Mount, they spot what at first seems to be a structure on the shore above a cliff.
As they get closer, they realize it is not a building at all.
It is the wreck of a ship smashed across rocks and twisted metal.
Then they hear a rhythmic thudding sound.
At first it sounds mechanical.
But as they close in, it becomes visceral and organic, like something roaring from deep in its throat.
The sound is coming from the far side of an encampment.
________________


Mara breaks
The moment Mara hears the sound clearly, everything about her changes.
She reaches for a dagger, but the one she threw earlier is gone.
So she draws her shortsword instead and sprints toward the encampment with unnatural, animal-like speed, dropping low as she runs.
The others follow as best they can.
Caspian’s familiar scouts ahead and reports:
* figures inside the encampment
* one figure standing on the wall, looking toward the source of the sound
Mara circles the encampment instead of charging straight through.
Then, suddenly, she reappears running away from the far side of it, clearly fleeing something.
Caspian calls after her, trying to help, but Mara shouts:
Mara:
 “Not another one of those. Go away.”
When Zaren closes the distance, he hears her repeating through tears:
Mara:
 “By the Four Nails. By the Four Nails. By the Four Nails.”
Her hand is shaking around her blade. Her face is pale and wet with tears.
She looks at him and says:
Mara:
 “Help them. Help them. Help them.”
Zaren asks how he can help effectively.
Mara, still breaking apart, answers:
Mara:
 “You will die. You will die. You will die. By the Four Nails.”
He asks for a weakness.
At last, she forces out the only useful answer she can manage:
Mara:
 “Crystals. Aim for the crystals.”
That is where the battle begins.
________________


The battle at the gate
At the start of the fight, Caspian moves to gain a better angle while his familiar returns to his shoulder so he can see through it more clearly.
From that vantage point, he sees the situation clearly:
* a large glass creature is battering a gate
* a small figure is nearby
* a man from inside the encampment is trying to defend the people behind the gate
The small figure vanishes and reappears repeatedly, moving by teleportation rather than normal motion.
The boy is speaking to Zaren, not to the whole party. He clearly thinks Mara has lost her mind.
Then the boy draws a metal shard or tool and strikes the large creature twice.
The hits matter. The creature visibly loses focus on the gate and begins to turn away from it.
Then the boy teleports again.
________________


Lysandra begins the counterattack
Lysandra acts next.
She places an area effect carefully, making sure not to catch the small figure.
From Zaren’s perspective, the ground begins glowing and dying under the creature as the effect spreads.
The creature keeps moving through the effect and continues taking damage from it.
________________


Caspian fires at the torso crystal
Then Caspian closes the distance enough to attack at range.
He studies the monster and asks which crystal is the main one.
The answer:
* the largest crystal is the white one in the torso, with purple around it
* the crystal in the head is entirely purple
* the rest of the body contains different colors
Caspian chooses the torso crystal.
His shoulder-mounted mechanism locks on with a laser.
He extends his arm and fires.
The shot is devastating, dealing an enormous hit of roughly fifty-five damage.
Then the device on his shoulder changes shape, sprouts spider-like legs, and flies forward under its own power to get closer to the fight.
________________


The child falls, the monster closes, and Zaren goes into melee
The creature survives Caspian’s blast and keeps fighting.
At one point it lashes out at the small teleporting figure and hurls him backward into a crate.
He drops unconscious immediately and begins dying.
Then the large creature performs a leaping slam, jumping thirty feet and crashing down near the party.
Zaren avoids the impact and takes no damage from the landing.
Now the creature is directly on top of him.
Zaren attacks in melee with his greatsword.
He targets the torso crystal first.
His hit lands in two parts:
* blunt force, which is not especially effective
* radiant damage, which lands much harder in the darkness
The creature reacts far more strongly to the radiant portion.
That confirms another crucial clue:
it is more vulnerable to radiant damage than to ordinary impact.
________________


The glass woman arrives
Lysandra keeps attacking from range, hiding in dim light, marking the target, and controlling its position.
Then, in the middle of the fight, a glass woman appears suddenly beside the unconscious child in a burst like shattering glass.
She kneels, lifts him up, and carries him.
This is clearly not random. She is reacting specifically to the fallen child.
Her arrival means the battle is tied to real people in the encampment, not just isolated monsters.
________________


Forced movement and the fall
Lysandra uses her force effect again, shoving the large creature toward the cliff edge.
She follows with another push and sends it farther off balance.
The creature is now on the verge of falling.
Her spider familiar closes in and bites it.
The venom matters. The creature shows visible signs of being affected by the poison.
Meanwhile, Caspian keeps pressure on it from range and asks the defenders to tell him its vulnerabilities.
Their answer matches Mara’s:
the crystals matter.
The large creature attacks again, trying to break through and kill whoever is nearest.
Zaren tries to overpower it physically and force it off balance, but it resists.
Alex, still farther from the main fight, continues attacking from range. One attack misses, another lands.
By this point the creature is visibly bloodied, with cracks spreading across its body, especially around the targeted crystal region.
________________


Silver, the cliff, and the stabilizing touch
The small teleporting fighter is identified by name:
Silver
He recovers just enough to act again and darts back in with impossible speed, sliding low and striking at the creature’s legs and heels, trying to destabilize it rather than simply wound it.
His hits glance off the hardened plating, but the intent is clear.
Lysandra attacks again with the staff, lands the hit, chooses the direction carefully, and finally sends the creature sideways off the cliff.
It falls onto another figure below, and both the falling damage and the impact damage land.
The large Refracted is now off the high ground.
At the same moment, Zaren tries to catch Silver with a reaction before the boy hits the ground, but Silver slips through his grasp.
Both Silver and the creature take the fall.
Silver drops unconscious.
Lysandra holds position rather than moving and risking retaliation.
Caspian sees Silver go down and immediately acts.
At first he thinks of sending his familiar to help normally, but then instinct takes over. While the familiar is touching Silver, he channels the strange energy building in the shield through it.
The result is unexpected.
The familiar opens, and two purple human-like fingers emerge from it and press against Silver’s face.
Silver stabilizes.
He is still unconscious, but no longer dying.
This is a major discovery for Caspian:
the shield can somehow channel restorative or preserving energy through the familiar, even though he does not yet fully understand how.
________________


The creature changes color before it dies
Caspian then rotates the turret and fires flame at the enemies below.
The smaller enemy is already badly hurt. The larger one is still bloodied, but not finished.
The spider familiar is pulled back to safer range.
Then the large creature below rises again and begins screaming.
Its color behavior changes in a very important way.
It tears part of itself free and raises it like a weapon.
As it does, something that had been white shifts to purple.
The change is obvious enough that the group notices immediately.
The color transition matters.
It likely reflects either a state change in the creature or a change in which crystal or energy source has become dominant.
________________


The encounter ends
The fight continues only briefly after that.
By now the defenders clearly have the advantage.
Between:
* the party’s focus fire
* Lysandra’s forced movement
* Zaren’s pressure in melee
* Caspian’s ranged damage
* Silver’s disruption
the creature is finally brought down.
That ends the encounter.
________________


Arrival inside the encampment
After the fight, the party reaches level 6.
They are brought fully inside the encampment.
Inside, they meet Elder Vorn.
The encampment contains around fifteen people in total.
About ten of them are lying on the ground, blue-skinned like corpses, unmoving except for the fact that they are still breathing.
They are gathered around a statue or central object.
They are not truly awake, but they are not dead either.
When someone asks about the glyphs, Silver proudly says:
Silver:
 “My work. I do that.”
Silver speaks like a child and an adult at once.
He looks no older than about ten, but his manner feels older.
Zaren introduces himself as:
Zaren:
 “Zaren of the Winged Watch.”
Silver thanks the party warmly and says he could not have kept going much longer. He says the monster had already knocked him out many times before they arrived.
The party now notices Silver’s glass more clearly:
* yellow glass like Lysandra’s on one side
* black glass like the others on the other side
* but unlike the party’s bonded shards, Silver’s glass appears to have grown from inside him
There are growths from his head and collarbones.
This is very different from the party’s own bondings.
________________


Mara enters, removes three nails, and disappears
Then Mara enters the encampment.
She comes over the wall without a word.
Her face is completely unreadable.
She walks straight to the center, removes three nails from the central post or pillar, puts them in her pocket, and disappears into her tent without explanation.
This immediately raises questions.
If a nail is placed whenever someone dies, or if the nails mark some other kind of bond, then removing three of them clearly means something important.
But no answer comes yet.
________________


Vorn writes their names onto himself
The situation in the encampment becomes clearer:
* Elder Vorn welcomes the party
* the glass woman who recovered Silver stands nearly motionless in the center
* Mara has withdrawn
* the ten blue people under the statue are alive, but barely
When asked if everyone here is one family, Vorn answers:
Vorn:
 “We are all what we have. Whoever is here is part of the family.”
Before the questions go too far, Caspian steps in and says plainly that they understand this is a difficult moment, but they know nothing and need answers.
Vorn asks:
Vorn:
 “Did Mara help you”
Caspian answers that she did.
Vorn asks each of them for their names.
They all give them.
Then Vorn opens his shirt.
Embedded in his chest is a purple crystal, very similar to Zaren’s. His body is covered in names, written directly on his skin.
He takes out a small coil or stylus and writes each of their names onto himself.
Then he says:
Vorn:
 “Now I will keep your names forever.”
There are dozens and dozens of names already written on his body, perhaps more than eighty.


Vorn begins explaining the encampment
Once the party is seated, Elder Vorn begins speaking more openly.
He says it is a difficult day, and that this is only the third time in his life that he has seen a creature like the one they just fought.
The last time, he says, Mara can tell them about it, not him.
When asked whether that is why Mara ran, Vorn says yes, but refuses to tell Mara’s secrets in her place. If she wants to speak, she will speak.
When asked whether creatures like that appear often, he says no. They are very rare. This is only the third time one of those monsters has forced them to move encampments.
________________


Vorn reveals who he is and how long he has been here
The party asks whether Vorn is one of the elders of the camp and whether he has been here a long time.
Vorn answers:
Vorn:
 “I am the only one in the camp. I am the only permanent one in the camp. I set up this camp thirty years ago to help people exiled from…”
He trails off, but the meaning is clear enough.
When asked what he means by permanent, he explains that everyone here is temporary. People come and go. People are short-lived in this land.
Then he speaks with brutal honesty about the others:
* Silver may only have about a year left
* Marisha, they do not know
* Mara, he would give five more years
* as for himself, he wakes every day expecting his end, and it never comes
This establishes the entire camp as a place of survival, not recovery.
________________


The truth about the blue bodies and the need for glass
The party pushes for the truth.
What exactly is happening here
What is the sickness in the land
Why do they need the glass
Are they all slowly dying
Vorn answers plainly:
Yes, they are slowly dying.
How quickly depends on how much glass they have bonded.
If the party had not bonded with glass, they would have ended up like the blue people lying on the ground, or worse.
He gestures toward the ten breathing bodies gathered around the central object and explains:
If they are found too late, they either become Refracted or become Hollow.
This confirms that the blue people are not a separate species. They are failed survivors.
________________


Mara tries to end the suffering of the blue ones
Caspian says something important at this point:
he needs Vorn’s help protecting the blue bodies from Mara.
Vorn understands immediately.
He explains that Mara tries to end one of them every day because she believes they are suffering.
If the party sees Mara near them, Vorn says, they should call for him.
This reveals something essential about Mara:
she is not simply hardened. She is living alongside the suffering every day and has begun treating mercy and killing as neighboring ideas.
________________


What the land is doing to them
When asked what is happening to the land and why everyone here is deteriorating, Vorn says it is the same story everyone knows:
the land is dead, and it is hungry for them.
The camp knows one practical truth above all others:
the glass helps them survive, but only prolongs their time.
This is not a permanent solution. It is an extension.
When asked why the glass works at all, Vorn explains:
Vorn:
 “It protects us from the land. The smog and the earth here are aggressive. They want to bond with your blood and consume it.”
That is the clearest statement yet of what Vastraya actually does:
the land itself is trying to bond with them.
The glass is a defensive compromise, not a cure.
________________


The Steel Grid and Vorn’s lost faith
Vorn identifies himself more fully:
Vorn:
 “Currently, I am Vorn of the Steel Grid.”
The party recognizes what that means.
The Steel Grid, or Order of the Steel Grip, follows Arcanon. It is associated with builders, architects, masons, archivists, and people who believe the world must be physically held together.
Its faith is structural and practical: the world does not remain whole through ideals alone. Someone has to build, reinforce, and bind it.
Vorn then explains:
* here, he is Vorn of the Steel Grid
* in the old world, he was an archivist in the Ministry of Sanctity
* he used to be a devout follower of Lunaria
* he lost all faith after what happened to him
________________


Caspian begins to realize his story may align with Vorn’s
Caspian tells Vorn that their stories may align.
He says that he was betrayed by Auditor Dominus, the Auditor Prime. He says he was a high-ranking researcher in the Ministry and had created an invention that was more sought after than he realized.
Vorn asks him:
Vorn:
 “Invention Are you an architect”
Caspian says yes.
Vorn answers immediately in practical terms:
Vorn:
 “Can you help us here”
Caspian says he will do his best.
Vorn tells him to speak with Charcy, who will explain what the camp needs, because their current situation is dire.
________________


House Aurelian and the theft of invention
Caspian continues his story.
He says a royal family was trying to get hold of his invention and ruined his reputation in order to seize his patents.
Vorn asks which family.
Caspian names House Aurelian.
Vorn recognizes the name and says they are a minor house, but an ambitious one.
Caspian says their ambition is dark. From his point of view, they tried to frame him as a heretic so they could take what he had built.
Vorn pushes back:
Vorn:
 “To be exiled, you must have done something.”
Caspian answers that, from his perspective, not really. They tried to paint him as a heretic.
Then Vorn shifts the conversation and asks a more pointed question:
Vorn:
 “What do you follow”
He wants to know Caspian’s path and faith, because if he truly was framed or truly saw something, that matters.
Caspian answers honestly that he used to follow Lunaria, but his faith is shaken now.
Vorn asks whether that break came because of exile, or whether it started before.
Caspian says it began after he started seeing what was really happening on the island.
Vorn presses him:
Vorn:
 “What have you seen”
Caspian says that he has not seen anything mystical in the obvious sense. Mostly politics, corruption, and what people do to each other.
Vorn answers with weary certainty:
Vorn:
 “Yes. It is always politics.”
________________


Zaren’s exile and chosen silence
The conversation shifts toward Zaren.
Vorn is surprised that a Marshal in the Guilded Guard now openly follows the Winged Watch.
He remarks that the island has become more progressive than he remembers, enough that he could almost consider going back.
Zaren confirms that in the empire now, the sons of the Church of the Sovereign Scars can follow different paths.
Vorn asks him:
Vorn:
 “Did you see something that got you exiled”
Zaren answers:
Zaren:
 “I was exiled by choice. And we’ll keep it at that.”
That answer closes the door rather than opening it.
________________


Mara whispers one word that changes everything
At that moment, Mara comes out and joins them.
She sits down with them carrying a crate, sets it down, and asks Vorn coldly:
Mara:
 “What lies has this one told you”
Vorn replies that he has not yet had the chance to hear any lies.
Mara leans close to Vorn and whispers something in his ear.
Zaren strains to hear.
He catches only one word:
“Children.”
Vorn’s reaction is immediate and intense.
He is visibly disgusted.
He physically gestures away from the party, specifically away from Caspian, as though wanting distance between them.
This matters because it suggests that one of them has just been linked, in Mara’s mind, to the Children of the Flame.
________________


Alex speaks with Silver about family
While that tension is unfolding, Alex turns to Silver and speaks to him more gently.
She asks what his connection to Mara is, and how he came to be here.
Silver says:
Silver:
 “Mara and Marisha have been taking care of me for the past ten years. I consider them my family.”
When asked about his parents, he says:
* he never knew his father
* his mother died giving birth to him
But he says it lightly, trying to make it sound fine:
Silver:
 “It’s all good, right I’m still living.”
Someone reassures him that it is all good.
Then Silver says something important:
family is not only blood.
There are other families in life.
At that exact moment, Alex hears a childlike voice in her head say:
Voice:
 “I like this one.”
It is not the spider familiar.
It is something else.
________________


Silver wants to go back, even though he has never been there
Alex asks Silver why they never tried to go back.
Silver says he has never gone back because he has never been there.
He has only heard stories.
He says the island sounds colorful and beautiful, and that he wishes he could see it.
Alex promises that if they find a way back, they will take him with them.
Then she asks Vorn directly:
Alex:

The Feast of the Nails
The party asks about the three nails Mara removed earlier.

* cleaning streets
* building things
* serving the community
The idea is that survival happens through chains, connection, and obligation.

Each household has a central post or structure. Driving the nail is a sign of belonging and standing within the chain.
The prayer behind it is essentially about not undoing the unmaking, but having the strength to stand upon its pieces.
The party realizes something important:

She carries their nails until she dies.
That means Mara’s chosen family includes Silver and two dead people whose memory she still physically bears.
The nails are not decoration. They are records of bond, belonging, shame, death, and obligation.

Vorn tells his own story

* how much was stored
* how much was consumed
* how much was distributed

The party realizes someone is sabotaging the aether system deliberately
