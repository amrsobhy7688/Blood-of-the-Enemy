Caspian realizes the statue is a construct, but not like the Cyriath ones
Caspian turns his full attention to the statue.
The moment he starts properly assessing it, an old idea snaps into place:
the statue is a construct, but not the same kind used in Cyriath.
The constructs in Cyriath are part of a closed patent, mass-produced by a workshop in Argenta, north of Cyriath. Caspian knows them well. He has even taken them apart before.
But this statue is different.
From his previous experience and current inspection, he knows:
* the Cyriath constructs work without visible aether crystals
* even when taken apart, their power source is not obvious
* this statue belongs to that same broad category of construct, but it is much older and cruder
* it has visible joints and layered stone plates
* one section is broken open, revealing internal mechanisms
* the internal mechanisms resemble the kind of gears and systems he has seen before
* yet there is still no visible power source
* there is no aether crystal inside
* there is no obvious engine or core
That is what begins to bother him.
The statue is functioning, or once functioned, as a powered construct, but its source of power is hidden or fundamentally different.

Inside the cave: the drowning ritual

* water
* suffocation
* submersion
